# ISTS JCC Digest — June 10, 2026

## Critical Developments

### CERC escalation and compensation orders issued for defaulting generators in multiple regions
CERC vide Order dated 02.03.2026 in Petitions 276/346/347/MP/2025 has directed compensation mechanisms for connectivity grantees whose projects have been revoked. Renew Green (MHP One) Pvt. Ltd. (Application 1200003881), Renew Green Energy Solutions Pvt. Ltd. (Applications 231400007, 231400011, 331400011), and Serentica Renewables India Pvt. Ltd. (Applications 431400004 & 412100015) were revoked on 10.03.2025. The Order establishes compensation rates of Rs 2,083 to Rs 2,773 per MW per day for delays and introduces monthly billing for unpaid compensation with LPS provisions. **So-what line**: Creates precedent for penalizing delayed generators while providing revenue stream to reduce transmission charges for all DICs under Sharing Regulations.

### CTUIL directed to offer withdrawal option for 60 GW of stalled connectivity applications  
CERC vide Order dated 10.04.2026 in Petition 666/MP/2025 (Saurya Urja Company vs CTUIL) directed CTUIL to provide withdrawal option to entities facing transmission planning difficulties affecting 60 GW of connectivity applications. Applicants can withdraw within two months of CTUIL communication with return of 50% application fees and all BGs. **So-what line**: Provides exit mechanism for projects stuck in planning bottlenecks while potentially freeing up transmission capacity for viable projects.

### Inquiry procedure for fraud/transgression in connectivity applications made effective  
CTUIL's blacklisting procedure effective from 09.10.2025 establishes formal process for investigating fraud in connectivity submissions. Entities can be debarred for 1-3 years for fraudulent documentation, with show cause procedures and personal hearings. **So-what line**: Creates regulatory teeth to penalize speculative or fraudulent applications, potentially improving quality of connectivity queue.

## Notable Updates

### Third amendment compliance deadlines approaching for LOA/PPA route applications
Applications under Regulation 37.10(a) where LOA/PPA quantum is less than installed capacity must submit additional details by 09.10.2025 or face processing under restrictive third amendment provisions. Separate compliance required under 37.10(d)(iii) by 09.11.2025 for entities with approved additional capacity lacking land documents. **So-what line**: Creates compliance deadline for developers to regularize applications or face capacity restrictions to LOA quantum only.

### Format standardization for BESS charging confirmation from RE developers  
CTUIL has issued format requiring developers to confirm commensurate REGS installation within 36 months for BESS projects. Three options provided: co-located RE for charging, new REGS for supply through ISTS, or charging from existing REGS. **So-what line**: Formalizes requirements for BESS-RE integration to prevent grid stability issues from standalone storage systems.

### Ownership disclosure mandate for all connectivity applicants implemented
CERC has directed collection of ownership/controlling shareholding details for all RE generators granted connectivity from 01.03.2021 onwards. Information required between application date and COD or 31.10.2024. **So-what line**: Enables tracking of beneficial ownership changes that may impact project viability or regulatory compliance.
