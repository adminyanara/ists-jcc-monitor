# ISTS JCC Digest — June 10, 2026

## Critical Developments

**Revised completion certificate process formalized for deemed COD claims under IEGC-2023**

CTUIL has published SOP Rev-2 establishing mandatory procedures for transmission licensees seeking completion certificates for ISTS elements that cannot achieve regular commercial operation despite being construction-complete. This addresses scenarios where generators or upstream/downstream transmission delays prevent service commencement. The SOP requires CEA energization approvals, GRID-INDIA no-load charging certificates (where possible), and CMD/CEO-level undertakings with full indemnification clauses. For TBCB projects, licensees must demonstrate completion per bidding guidelines and TSA specifications; for RTM projects, compliance with CTU's OM/NCT letters or MoP directives. **So-what: This creates a standardized path for transmission licensees to claim deemed COD and begin tariff recovery even when external dependencies prevent actual service, reducing regulatory uncertainty for project financing.**
