## Critical Developments

### Policy / Procedural Shift: CTUIL Formalises Completion Certificate Issuance Process for Deemed COD Claims (TBCB and RTM Projects)

**Synthesis:** CTUIL has issued SOP Rev-2 (dated 18 July 2025) standardising the process by which transmission licensees may obtain a CTU Completion Certificate — a prerequisite for either (a) approaching CERC for COD approval under the Regulated Tariff Mechanism (RTM), or (b) self-declaring deemed COD under the Tariff Based Competitive Bidding (TBCB) route. The SOP operationalises the fifth proviso to Regulation 27(1)(c)(i) of IEGC-2023, which protects transmission licensees from commercial exposure where their assets are ready but commissioning is blocked by delays in generating stations or upstream/downstream transmission systems of other licensees. The SOP requires licensees to write to HoD (PMG), CTUIL with: (i) element-wise completion details exactly matching TSA/OM nomenclature, (ii) CEA energisation approval for each element, (iii) GRID-INDIA no-load charging certificate or a written explanation for its absence, and (iv) a board-level undertaking (CMD/CEO/MD/Director rank; Executive Director acceptable for RTM PSUs) certifying conformity with CEA Technical Standards — specifically the 2022 Construction Standards, 2007 Connectivity Standards, 2020 Communication Standards, and 2023 Safety Regulations. Separate undertaking formats are prescribed for TBCB and RTM projects (Annexure-A). The SOP explicitly requires that no element in the application may depend on an incomplete element within the same project scope.

**Region(s):** All regions (pan-India applicability)

**Meeting reference:** Not a JCC meeting document — standalone SOP dated 18-07-2025, published on CTUIL website.

**Key entities:** CTUIL (HoD, PMG division); all ISTS transmission licensees (TBCB and RTM); GRID-INDIA (RLDC no-load charging certificates); CEA (energisation approvals); CERC (downstream recipient of COD petitions under RTM route).

**So-what line:** This SOP creates a mandatory procedural checklist — with board-level accountability — that every transmission licensee must satisfy before CTUIL will issue the certificate needed to protect tariff entitlements when commissioning delays are attributable to generators or other licensees, making non-compliance a direct financial risk for any licensee whose ready assets are sitting idle.
