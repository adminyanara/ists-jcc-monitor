# ISTS JCC Digest — June 15, 2026

## Critical Developments

**New streamlined procedure for CTUIL completion certificates enables faster deemed COD declarations for transmission licensees**

CTUIL has issued a revised Standard Operating Procedure (Rev-2) dated July 18, 2025, establishing clear requirements for transmission licensees to obtain completion certificates under IEGC-2023 Regulation 27(1)(c)(i). This addresses situations where transmission systems are ready for service but cannot commence commercial operations due to delays in upstream/downstream systems or associated generators — not attributable to the transmission licensee. The SOP creates a standardized process requiring transmission licensees to submit applications to HoD (PMG), CTUIL with CEA energization approvals, GRID-INDIA no-load charging certificates (where applicable), and undertakings signed by CMD/CEO/MD/Director level executives. For TBCB projects, this enables deemed COD declarations per TSA provisions; for RTM projects, it supports CERC petitions for COD approval. The procedure applies to both individual elements and partial project completions where utilization doesn't depend on incomplete components within the same project scope.

**Region(s)**: All regions (pan-India procedural change)  
**Meeting reference(s)**: SOP document dated 18-07-2025  
**Key entities**: CTUIL, all transmission licensees (TBCB and RTM), CERC, CEA, GRID-INDIA  
**So-what line**: This creates a clear regulatory pathway for transmission licensees to monetize completed assets even when dependent infrastructure is delayed, reducing revenue losses and improving project economics.
