# ISTS JCC Digest — 2026-06-11

## Critical Developments

**CTUIL streamlines deemed COD process with revised completion certificate SOP**
CTUIL has issued SOP Rev-2 (dated 18-07-2025) implementing new procedures for transmission licensees to obtain completion certificates under IEGC-2023 Regulation 27(1)(c)(i). The SOP enables transmission licensees to declare deemed COD when ISTS elements are complete but prevented from regular service due to delays in connected generating stations or upstream/downstream transmission systems not attributable to the licensee. Key procedural changes include mandatory CEO/CMD/Director-level undertakings for TBCB projects, required CEA energization approvals, and GRID-INDIA no-load charging certificates where applicable. The process applies to both TBCB projects (requiring TSA compliance) and RTM projects (requiring CTU OM/NCT letter/MoP OM compliance).

**Region(s)**: All regions (Pan-India procedural change)
**Meeting reference(s)**: Not applicable (Standalone SOP document)
**Key entities**: CTUIL, all transmission licensees, CERC, CEA, GRID-INDIA
**So-what line**: This creates a standardized compliance pathway for transmission licensees to recover costs on stranded assets when delays are due to external factors, potentially reducing regulatory disputes over COD declarations.
