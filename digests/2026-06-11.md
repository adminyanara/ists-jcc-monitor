# ISTS JCC Digest — June 11, 2026

## Critical Developments

**New deemed COD certification pathway formalized for ISTS projects facing upstream/downstream delays**

CTUIL has issued revised Standard Operating Procedure (SOP Rev-2) dated July 18, 2025, operationalizing IEGC-2023 Regulation 27(1)(c)(i) provisions that allow transmission licensees to obtain deemed commercial operation dates when their completed ISTS elements cannot enter regular service due to delays in connected generating stations or other transmission systems. The procedure requires transmission licensees to submit completion applications to HoD (PMG), CTUIL with CEA energization approvals, GRID-INDIA no-load charging certificates (where applicable), and CMD/CEO/MD-level undertakings certifying conformity with CEA standards. For TBCB projects, the certificate enables deemed COD declaration per TSA provisions without waiting for upstream/downstream readiness. This creates a regulatory pathway for transmission licensees to recover tariffs on stranded but completed assets while generators or other transmission elements face commissioning delays. The procedure shifts commercial risk allocation by allowing partial project monetization when delays are not attributable to the transmission licensee.

**Region(s)**: All regions (pan-India procedure)  
**Meeting reference(s)**: SOP issued July 18, 2025 implementing IEGC-2023  
**Key entities**: CTUIL, all transmission licensees (PGCIL, private TBCB developers), CEA, GRID-INDIA, CERC  
**So-what line**: This procedure enables transmission licensees to secure deemed COD and start tariff recovery on completed assets even when connected generators or upstream transmission face commissioning delays, reducing stranded asset exposure.
